Batch Thumbnail Shooting Guide

Shoot once, use for months. Capture everything our team needs to create high-converting thumbnails for all your upcoming content — without needing a new photoshoot for every single video.

Why Batch Shooting

Instead of taking a new photo every time a video goes out, we ask clients to do one dedicated thumbnail session covering a range of outfits, expressions, and angles. This gives our design team a full library to pull from — meaning faster turnaround, more consistency, and zero delays waiting on new photos.

Before You Shoot

Block out 30-45 minutes

This session takes a bit longer than a normal filming day since you'll be changing outfits and running through multiple expressions — but it saves you from ever having to do this again for months.

Use your regular studio setup

Same lighting, same background, same camera position as your normal videos — this keeps your thumbnails visually consistent with your content.

Shoot in photo mode, not video

If your camera supports high-resolution photo mode, use that instead of pulling stills from video — photos will look sharper and cleaner when zoomed in or cropped for thumbnail use.

Outfits — Bring 3-4 Options

Change into a different outfit for each round of shots. Suggested variety:

01

Your primary look

Structured button-up or the outfit you wear most often on camera.

02

A blazer or formal layer

For higher-stakes or "announcement" style thumbnails.

03

Different color / casual

A slightly more casual version — gives variety so thumbnails don't all look identical.

04

Campaign color (optional)

Any specific color that matches an upcoming campaign, offer, or seasonal theme.

Expressions & Poses — Capture All of These

For every outfit, run through this full list of expressions before moving to the next outfit:

Neutral / confident

Calm, composed, direct eye contact with the camera.

Slight smile

Approachable but still credible, not exaggerated.

Serious / focused

Brows slightly furrowed, communicates authority — great for "mistake," "warning," or contrarian topic thumbnails.

Pointing gesture

Hand pointing toward the camera or to the side — useful for instructional or "here's how" style thumbnails.

Arms crossed

Confident, authoritative pose — great for credibility-driven thumbnails.

Hand near chin / thinking

Useful for "question" or "the truth about" style topics.

Looking slightly off-camera

Works well for thumbnails with bold text taking up the main visual space.

Angles to Capture

For each expression above, quickly capture:

Straight-on, centered

The standard framing — face square to the camera.

Slightly angled

Turn 10-15 degrees left or right — gives our design team flexibility for different compositions.

Practical Tips

Take multiple shots of each pose

Don't just take one photo per expression — take 5-6 rapid shots of each so we have options if your eyes are closed, expression looks off, or focus is slightly soft in any single frame.

Keep your energy consistent throughout

It's a long session — don't let your energy visibly drop in later outfits. Take a short break between outfit changes if needed so every set looks equally sharp and engaged.

Avoid busy patterns or logos

Stick to solid colors or subtle textures — anything with a busy pattern or another brand's logo will distract from the thumbnail's focal point, which should always be your face and expression.

Send us everything, not just your favorites

Upload the full batch of photos rather than pre-selecting — our design team is trained to spot what works best for thumbnail composition, and sometimes a shot that doesn't feel like a "favorite" in isolation works perfectly once text and color grading are added.

What to Send Us

  • All the photos from the session, organized by outfit in separate folders
  • Uploaded to your shared Google Drive thumbnail folder
  • Let us know in Slack once the upload is complete
THE RESULT

One session like this typically gives our team enough material to create thumbnails for 2-3 months of content without ever needing to schedule another photoshoot — saving you significant time while keeping every thumbnail looking fresh, varied, and on-brand.
