The Process, Step by Step

Step 2

Slack Setup

Slack is our primary communication channel. All day-to-day communication, file sharing, feedback, and updates happen here — not via email.

What to expect:

Step 3

Kickoff Call

Your kickoff call happens within 2 business days of your payment confirmation. This is the most important call of the entire engagement — come prepared.

What we cover on this call:
  • Your business — what you sell, who you sell it to, current revenue, and growth goals
  • Your offer — we need to fully understand what you are selling before we can build content around it
  • Your audience — who your ideal customer is, their pain points, and what makes them buy
  • Your current content situation — what you have tried, what has worked, what has not
  • Equipment and recording setup — we will review your current gear and advise on anything that needs improving before filming begins
  • Platform access — we will confirm which platforms you are on and set up access for our team
  • Communication preferences — how often you want updates and in what format
This call can run up to 60 minutes. The more detail you give us here, the better everything that follows will be. Do not rush this call.
Please have ready before this call:
  • A clear description of your offer and who it is for
  • Your current monthly revenue (approximate is fine)
  • Examples of content you like — YouTube channels, creators, or brands whose style you want to emulate
  • Any existing brand assets — logo, colors, fonts
  • Login access to your YouTube channel and any social platforms you are currently using
Step 4

Offer & Strategy Alignment

This is the step most agencies skip entirely — and it is exactly why most content gets views but no clients. Before a single video gets filmed, we align your offer with your content strategy.

What happens in this step:
  • We review your current offer and give you direct feedback on how to position it for your target audience
  • We build detailed avatars of your ideal customer — who they are, what they struggle with, what makes them buy
  • We align every content idea with your specific offer so every video you film has a clear business purpose
  • We identify the content pillars that will attract your avatar and repel everyone else
  • We agree on a consistent voice and tone for your content that matches your brand positioning
The narrower your offer and the more specifically your content speaks to your avatar, the higher your conversion rate will be. A video with 500 views from the right audience will generate more revenue than a video with 50,000 views from the wrong one.
Step 5

Asset Collection

Before production begins, we need everything in one place. Missing assets are the most common reason for delayed timelines — please prioritize getting these to us quickly.

Assets we need from you:
  • Brand logo (PNG with transparent background preferred)
  • Brand colors (hex codes if available)
  • Brand fonts (or your preferences if not defined yet)
  • Any existing content you have published (so we understand your style)
  • Examples of thumbnails, channels, or brands you like visually
  • Access to your YouTube channel (Manager access — not ownership)
  • Access to your Instagram, TikTok, Twitter/X accounts (where relevant to your tier)
  • Your website URL and any existing sales page copy
All assets should be sent via your dedicated Slack channel, not email. Use a shared Google Drive folder if files are large.
Step 6

Content Calendar

Once your strategy is aligned and assets are collected, we build your first monthly content calendar. This is the blueprint for everything that gets produced.

What the content calendar includes:
  • YouTube long-form video topics for the month (with suggested titles)
  • Short-form content plan — which clips to pull from each long-form video
  • Platform-specific posting schedule (YouTube, Instagram, TikTok, Twitter/X, LinkedIn)
  • Newsletter topic aligned to each week's video
  • Any community posts or Discord content tied to the weekly theme
Your responsibilities:
  • Review and approve the content calendar before production begins
  • Flag any topics that feel off-brand or misaligned with your offer
  • Suggest any timely topics, upcoming launches, or seasonal angles to incorporate
The content calendar is a living document — it gets reviewed and adjusted monthly based on what is performing and what your audience is responding to.
Step 7

Scripting Session

We do not write your scripts for you — your content needs to be in your voice, not ours. What we do is help you structure your ideas, sharpen your hooks, and make sure every script is aligned with your offer and your avatar before you film.

How scripting works:
  • You write a rough draft or talking points — use voice-to-text if typing feels slow
  • Send your draft to us via Slack
  • We review it and send back specific notes on hook strength, pacing, offer alignment, and any gaps
  • You revise and confirm — we do a final check before you film
Do not wait until your script is perfect before sending it to us. A rough draft with your real ideas is more useful than a polished version that took three times as long.
Step 8

Filming & Raw Footage

Once your script is approved, you film. Here is everything you need to know to give us the best possible raw footage to work with.

Recording checklist:
  • Film in the highest resolution your camera supports — 4K preferred
  • Export audio as 32-bit float WAV at 48,000 Hz if using a dedicated microphone (Shure SM7B or equivalent)
  • Make sure your lighting is set up correctly before pressing record — check a test frame on your phone or monitor
  • Film in a quiet space — turn off fans, AC, notifications, and anything that creates background noise
  • Film a few seconds of room silence at the start — this gives us a noise profile for audio cleanup
  • Do not stop filming if you make a mistake — just pause, take a breath, and redo the line. We cut it in editing
  • If you stumble on a section, flag the timestamp in Slack so we know where to look
How to send us your footage:
  • Upload raw footage files to the shared Google Drive folder in your Slack channel
  • Label files clearly — e.g., 'Video1_RawFootage_Date'
  • Include any B-roll, screen recordings, or supporting clips in a separate folder labeled 'B-Roll'
  • Let us know in Slack when the upload is complete
The faster you get us raw footage, the faster your content goes live. Our production timeline starts from the moment we receive your footage, not from the moment you finish filming.
Step 9

Production & Review

Once we have your raw footage, our editing team takes over. Here is what happens during production and how the review process works.

Production timeline:
  • First draft delivered within 3-5 business days of receiving footage (Full Brand Partner tier: 24-48 hours)
  • Thumbnails delivered alongside the first draft
  • Short-form clips pulled and formatted for each platform
Review process:
  • Watch the full edit before giving feedback — do not send notes halfway through
  • Consolidate all feedback into one Slack message per revision round — not multiple separate messages
  • Be specific — 'the intro feels slow' is more useful than 'I don't like it'
  • We allow up to 2 revision rounds per video — additional rounds may incur extra time
  • Thumbnail feedback — let us know if you want to try an alternate version for A/B testing (Growth and Full Brand Partner tiers)
Our editing style is premium and calm — no rapid jump cuts, no meme sounds, no hype edits. Every video is built to attract and retain your specific avatar, not to go viral with a general audience.
Step 10

Publishing & Repurposing

Once your video is approved, we handle publishing and repurposing across all platforms included in your tier.

What we handle:
  • Upload to YouTube with optimized title, description, tags, and chapters
  • Schedule short-form clips to Instagram, TikTok, Twitter/X, and LinkedIn (based on your tier)
  • Platform-specific captions written for each short — not just the same caption copy-pasted everywhere
  • Community post in Discord tied to the week's video topic (if applicable)
  • Newsletter draft based on the video sent to you for review before sending (based on your tier)
We use Later for scheduling. You will be given view access to the Later calendar so you can see exactly what is going out and when at all times.
Step 11

Email Marketing Setup & Management

Email is the most underused and highest-converting tool in organic marketing. Unlike social media, your email list is an asset you own — no algorithm can take it away. We help you set it up, grow it, and use it to consistently nurture your audience into paying clients.

What we help you set up (if not already done):
  • Email platform setup — we recommend Kit (formerly ConvertKit) for its automation and segmentation capabilities
  • Custom domain email sending (e.g., newsletter@yourdomain.com) so your emails land in the inbox, not spam
  • Landing page to collect subscribers — connected to your website and linked in every video description
  • Welcome sequence — a 3-5 email automated series that every new subscriber receives, building trust from day one before you ever send a campaign
  • Newsletter template — branded to your colors and fonts so every email looks professional and consistent
Ongoing email management (based on your tier):
  • Weekly email written and sent to your list — repurposed from that week's YouTube video with one exclusive insight not in the video
  • Every email revolves around your offer — content that educates, entertains, and consistently points back to what you sell
  • Subject line testing — we test different subject lines to improve open rates over time
  • List segmentation — tagging subscribers based on behavior so you can send more targeted messages as your list grows
  • Monthly email performance report — open rates, click rates, unsubscribes, and revenue attributed to email
The goal of your email list is not to send pretty newsletters — it is to create a direct line to your most engaged audience so that when you make an offer, the right people are already warm and ready to say yes. Most of our clients see their email list become their highest-converting sales channel within 60-90 days of consistent sending.
Step 12

Community Building

A community is the single most powerful nurturing tool available to a personal brand. It turns passive viewers into active participants, builds deep trust before any sales conversation happens, and creates a network effect where your audience starts selling your offer to each other.

Platform options:
  • Discord — our recommendation for most clients. Free to build, excellent for real-time engagement, free coaching calls, resource sharing, and building genuine relationships.
  • Skool — better for structured course-based communities where members progress through content. Paid platform but highly polished.
We will recommend the right platform for you based on your audience and offer during your kickoff call.
What we help you build:
  • Full community setup — channels, categories, roles, rules, and branding all configured before your first member joins
  • Welcome flow — every new member gets a clear, warm welcome message explaining what the community is and what to do first
  • Content structure — dedicated channels for resources, wins, Q&A, announcements, and general conversation
  • Engagement strategy — a weekly rhythm of posts, prompts, and calls that keeps the community active
  • Free coaching call setup — weekly or bi-weekly calls hosted inside the community where you deliver value
  • Resource library — a dedicated space for templates, swipe files, guides, and frameworks members can access anytime
What we need from you: confirmation of which platform you want to use, any existing community you have started (we will audit and rebuild it), and commitment to showing up for the weekly coaching calls — your personal presence is the most valuable thing in the community, especially early on.
